GlglStats

GlglStats

GlglStats is a novel approach to measuring musical repetitiveness through compression techniques.

The core idea is leveraging the LZ77 compression algorithm to quantify how predictable or repetitive a song is. The more repetitive the song, the higher its compression rate.

This project provides an objective metric for what makes songs "catchy" or "repetitive" - terms that are usually subjectively defined.

The tool analyzes songs and generates visual representations of their repetitive structures, making patterns in music visible and comparable.

PyData TLV Conference Presentation

The presentation explores how compression algorithms can be used to analyze music complexity and repetitiveness.

Parking Heat Map Visualization

This project created a dynamic heat map showing parking availability throughout Tel Aviv neighborhoods, based on crowdsourced data from local residents.

The visualization helped identify patterns in parking availability throughout different times of day and days of the week, making it easier for residents to plan their commutes.

Data collection was facilitated through a community-driven approach, with residents sharing real-time parking information through a dedicated platform.
